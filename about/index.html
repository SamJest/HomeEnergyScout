About the project

Home Energy Scout is built to make everyday home energy questions easier to answer.

The site focuses on practical UK household decisions such as running costs, likely savings, room sizing, and direct comparisons between common options.

What the site is for

Home Energy Scout is for people who want a quick, sensible answer to ordinary household energy questions without having to wade through vague blog posts, supplier sales pages, or long forum threads.

The aim is to help with practical questions such as:

  • what an appliance or heater costs to run
  • whether a change is likely to save money
  • what size heater, radiator, or dehumidifier may suit a room
  • which of two common options is likely to cost less overall

Useful routes

  • Running costsThe core answer hub for cost questions.
  • Savings and paybackUpgrade and replacement decisions with practical framing.
  • MethodologyHow the estimates are built and what they should be treated as.

What the site is not trying to do

  • It is not trying to replace a proper installer quote.
  • It is not trying to chase news or short-lived trends.
  • It is not built as a generic green-lifestyle blog.
  • It is not meant to overwhelm people with unnecessary technical detail.

How the site should feel

  • plain-English, not salesy
  • quick to use on a phone
  • clear about assumptions
  • useful even when the answer is only a rough estimate
Important: Home Energy Scout is intended as a practical estimate and decision-support site. It should help users narrow down options and understand likely costs, but it does not provide supplier quotes, installation advice, or a substitute for professional assessment where one is needed.
