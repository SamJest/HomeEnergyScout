# Project plan

## Brand direction
**Home Energy Scout**

A descriptive but scalable brand for a UK-first household energy answer site focused on running costs, savings, sizing and practical comparison decisions.

## Product goal
Build a static, evergreen, tool-first website that helps ordinary UK households answer practical questions such as:
- What does this cost to run?
- How much could I save?
- What size do I need?
- Which option is cheaper?
- Is it worth upgrading or replacing this?

## Core pillars
1. Running costs
2. Savings and payback
3. Sizing and usage
4. Comparisons and decisions

## Delivery status
The initial 10-batch build is now complete.

### Delivered in the launch scope
- homepage
- 5 live section hubs
- trust/legal pages
- 6 core calculators
- 24+ supporting pages
- shared design system and JS layer
- metadata, schema, tracking and sitemap foundations
- root-ready static structure suitable for Codex follow-on work

## Ongoing direction for future expansion
- deepen the strongest UK clusters before widening topic breadth too far
- keep pages tool-first and answer-first rather than drifting into generic content
- add new calculators through shared config patterns where possible
- preserve strong internal links between costs, sizing, savings and compare routes
- keep future country expansion architectural, not content-led, until a full localisation pass is justified
