What makes a comparison useful

  • Both options must be solving roughly the same problem to the same standard.
  • Comfort, drying speed and convenience can outweigh a small difference in running cost.
  • A cheap answer is not a good answer if it leaves the room cold, damp or inconvenient to live with.

Comparison FAQ

Use these answers when two options look close on paper but the real choice still feels unclear.

Why is the cheapest option not always the best comparison result?

Because the cheapest option can still be wrong for the job. If it leaves a room cold, dries clothes too slowly, or creates a damp problem, a slightly higher running cost may still be the better household choice.

Should I get a baseline running cost before comparing?

Usually yes. A baseline cost or sizing check helps you compare on the right terms instead of treating unlike situations as if they were directly equivalent.
