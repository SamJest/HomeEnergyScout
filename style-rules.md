# Style Rules

## Voice
- UK English
- plain, practical, confident
- no hype
- no filler intros
- no fake expert language
- no generic “in today’s world” phrasing
- no bloated lifestyle framing

## Page behaviour
- answer first
- assumptions visible
- examples realistic
- next useful link obvious
- mobile-first layout
- ad-ready without feeling built around ads

## Homepage rules
- explain the promise quickly
- route users into live sections
- keep trust visible
- avoid vague feature blocks that do not lead anywhere

## Hub page rules
- quick answer near the top
- make topic clusters feel useful, not decorative
- explain what the section is for
- provide at least one route to each relevant neighbouring section
- include a practical “what to do next” module

## Copy clean-up checks
Before delivery:
- remove “the site is being built to...” language where a page can now speak in the present
- remove placeholder-sounding wording unless the feature truly is not live yet
- avoid repetitive section intros
- tighten weak or generic summaries
- prefer practical nouns and verbs over abstract phrasing

## Batch 2 update
The homepage and hubs now speak more in the present. Future batches should continue removing any remaining “planned” or “future” language as live calculators and support pages are added.


## Final handoff note
Future additions should preserve the current plain, practical tone and avoid generic intros, lifestyle framing or sales-style persuasion.
