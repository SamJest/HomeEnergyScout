Quick answer

A dehumidifier is often cheaper to run than extra direct electric heating when the main job is managing moisture or helping clothes dry indoors. The real question is not only the cost, but whether the unit is the right size and whether a drying route like a heated airer or tumble dryer would suit the job better.

Use the rated wattage if you know it. Clothes-drying and persistent-damp patterns often run for longer than people first assume.

Think about the job, not just the appliance

A dehumidifier used for laundry drying behaves differently from one left on to keep a chronically damp room under control. Use the mode that best matches the real job.
